Materials

  • 10 ml of dish soap
  • 100 ml of cold water
  • 400 ml of white vinegar
  • Food coloring
  • Baking soda slurry (fill a cup about ½ with baking soda, then fill the rest of the way with water)
  • Empty 2 liter soda bottle

Volvano Instructable

Step 1 - Combine the vinegar, water, dish soap and 2 drops of food coloring into the empty soda bottle.

Does the amount of vinegar change the eruption?

Step 2 - Use a spoon to mix the baking soda slurry until it is all a liquid.

Carbon dioxide is the same type of gas used to make the carbonation in sodas. What happens if you shake up a soda?

Step 3 - Eruption time! … Pour the baking soda slurry into the soda bottle quickly and step back!

The gas gets very excited and tries to spread out. There is not enough room in the bottle for the gas to spread out so it leaves through the opening very quickly, causing an eruption!.
